STB India Travel Trade Connection Newsletter
Singapore Tourism Board (STB) partnered with St+art India Foundation (St+art) to unveil a collaborative artwork in January, at Little India, Singapore.
The unique mural artwork titled, “Dancing in Unison” was the result of a shared passion between Indian Gond artist Padma Shri Bhajju Shyam and Singaporean artist Sam Lo. It is among the tallest art murals in the city. The mural was the result of many months of planning where both the artists had connected virtually to create this composition. Due to Covid-19 restrictions, it was painted solely by Sam Lo. This mural was one of the key highlights of Artwalk 2021 themed “In Spite Of” - where artists overcame limitations and geographical boundaries to come together to realise their creations, made possible by their passions and hopes for harmony for the future.

3. STB INDIA ORGANISED ITS FIRST VIRTUAL TRADESHOW SERIES
STB India organised its first Virtual Trade-show series, under the theme “Charting Our Pathways to Tourism Recovery”, over 4 days in January for South, North, West and East India regions. DDP Publications India was our Event Organiser. Over 45 Singapore tourism stakeholders (comprising airlines, attractions, cruise, DMCs and hotels) attended the event. Out of 3,200 registered buyers for the Trade-show, a total of 2,5200 buyers logged in from all over India and each booth had an average of 400 unique visitors.

The Virtual Trade-show was aimed to update the Trade fraternity about refreshed tourism offerings and experiences as well as new norms adopted by Singapore as part of Safety Management Measures to manage the COVID-19 pandemic and prepare for re-start of tourism.
4. STB INDIA PARTNERS WITH NATIONAL GEOGRAPHIC TO CREATE 8 VIDEOS
This was a first-of-its kind partnership between STB and National Geographic India, aimed at connecting people with Singapore and igniting their passions to travel when borders re-open. The 8 videos showed the city through the lens of 4 Indian personalities (photographer, installation artist, explorer, and chef) and another 4 inspirational short clips through visual montages.

A series of 4 advocacy films were shot in India with arts, photography, culinary and urban greenery as subject heads and anchored by Indian KOLs – street artist Hanif Qureshi, acclaimed photographer Samar Jodha, author and baker Shivesh Bhatia and Nat Geo explorer Eshika Fyzee. The personalities shared about their passions, their connection with Singapore and what they look forward to most in their next visit to SG. The second series of 4 shorter clips take audience through a journey in Singapore to find diversity and marvel at the right balance of modernity, greenery, and culture. The 8 films went live in Feb 2021 and were released exclusively on National Geographic India’s social media platforms which have a reach of over 10 million fans. They were promoted on Nat Geo social and TV channel and had garnered almost 4million impressions in the first 1.5 month.

6. CELEBRATING SG FOOD THRU VIRTUAL CULINARY EVENT WITH MAI BAO RESTAURANT
STB invited some of New Delhi’s top food influencers and media publications to a media engagement event at Mai Bao on 12 March 2021, a new and modern Asian street food restaurant at DLF Avenue Saket, one of the biggest and most well-known commercial malls in New Delhi. While Mai Bao’s menu is Asian, it has a significant selection of Singapore dishes. Carefully curated by Singaporean celebrity chef Jeremy Nguee, who had trained the Mai Bao team for more than a year, the menu includes Nasi Lemak, Singapore Chilli Crab, Laksa, Hokkien Prawn Mee, Satay, Hainanese Chicken Rice and Salted Egg Prawns. Positioned as a modern Singapore street food restaurant, Mai Bao is arguably the first restaurant of its kind in New Delhi that serves up a good selection of Singaporean local cuisine.

At the virtual event, Chef Jeremy shared his culinary journey and experience working with Mai Bao. The main highlight was a live demonstration conducted by the Chef in his Singaporean residence; where he made 3 Singaporean delights - Popiah, Meat Bao and Ondeh Ondeh (warm). The session was streamed live to the audience via Zoom who had gathered at Mai Bao. The participants were happy to whip up the dishes, learning and interacting with Chef Jeremy.
7. STB INDIA’S MICE INITIATIVES
> CORPORATE ENGAGEMENT THROUGH VIRTUAL LUNCHEON

STB India, in partnership with OptiMICE Events, organised a 90-minute exclusive interactive Virtual Luncheon on 27th Feb. Under the theme of “Charting Our Pathways to MICE Recovery”, the event brought together more than 30 distinguished Corporate decision makers from Delhi and Kolkata in the MICE industry to gain insights on how major Indian corporations are reviewing their MICE activities and how Singapore is preparing the way forward in 2021 and beyond. The event saw a robust discussion and attendance by corporate leaders from companies from diverse industry verticals, including larger conglomerates, banking and finance, insurance, and cement. The event concluded with a virtual lunch which was delivered to everybody’s homes from Qmin Taj with utmost level of safety and hygiene.

> VIRTUAL FOOTBALL TOURNAMENT WITH JTB INDIA

STB was the Exclusive Country Partner for a Virtual Football Tournament organised by JTB India Pvt. Ltd., a key player in the M&I space, in mid Feb. This one of its kind creative outreach effort enabled STB to stay connected with the MICE corporates through key MICE intermediary.

The tournament was played on PlayStation4 and drew an overwhelming participation by 128 players from 58 Corporates. The participants from various corporate segments were formed into 32 groups of 4 players each. The total number of matches played in a league format was 192 and each player had the chance to play at least 3 matches. The top three winners were awarded gift vouchers.
